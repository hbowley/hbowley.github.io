## The Short Version

I'm a data scientist and biostatistician based in Miami. I lead the Research and Data Analytics team at Miami Dade College, and I'm Principal Data Scientist at Portable Networks where I build custom BI solutions for clients. I have my MPH in Biostatistics from Florida International University.

## How I Got Here

My path wasn't linear, and that's what makes me good at this. I started in psychology research at Florida State — running rodent spatial navigation experiments and doing spike sorting in MATLAB. That led me to FIU, where I spent four years on NIH-funded neurodevelopmental research, including the ABCD Study. I processed neuroimaging data, built statistical workflows for propensity score matching and multilevel modeling, and learned what rigorous research actually looks like.

Along the way, I got my MPH in Biostatistics — my practicum involved analyzing the effects of stimulant medication on brain development using diffusion-weighted neuroimaging data. I then coordinated the Project Seahorse study, examining hippocampal structure and spatial ability, and started building machine learning models for spatial language analysis.

From there, I moved into consulting — building Shiny apps on AWS with PostgreSQL backends for client-facing analytics — and eventually into leading institutional research at Miami Dade College, where I manage a team of data professionals and build ML models for student outcomes.

## What I'm Working On Now
